Background

Najarin is an OverWorld Muge who serves Maxxor as one of his Counsellors, along with Intress.

Appearance

Najarin is depicted with many of the aspects of Merlin, the Wizard of the Legend of Arthur.

He’s tall and very thin with light blue skin, hands with long fingers with long and sharp nails. On his stern face with sunken cheeks, triangle-shaped eyes with a small azure-cyan iris, a large, thick nose and a very long white moustache that grows to extend to a very subtle platinum grey beard. He wears a sleeveless high neck blue shirt with prominent shoulder pads that holds a long and discretely decorated blue cape. A long and pointy ornamented hat, dark-purple short trousers that reveals bandaged legs and pointy shoes common to fantasy magical creatures finish his clothing.

Najarin uses heavy blue makeup around the eyes and on his eyebrows and black all over his eyelids, similar to an Egyptian Pharaoh. He may also look a bit ostentatious with golden bracelets and ruby and emerald rings on his index and middle fingers of both hands.

In the Animation, starting with the M’arrillian Invasion, his overall appearance became softer but without losing his sternness, his cheeks are now less sunken, his nose better shaped, lighter makeup and his clothing less pompous, without the rings and the old opulent bracelets replaced with more simpler ones, but yet, he got himself a golden armlet added to his left arm.

Personality & Behaviour

Najarin is probably one of the oldest Creatures alive in Perim and one of the wisest among the OverWorlders and as such he’s a superb Strategist, serving to the rulers of his Tribe as Counsellor since the early days of Kiru City. He often may not seem to be very kind, but he’s always respectful to everyone, except with those who trespasses his Castle at Lake Ken-I-Po, where he lives secluded.

His wisdom also makes him one of the first Creatures to understand the concepts of Chaotic. As shown in Maze of Menace, he knew that the “devices” used by the players to play allow them to transport themselves across Perim in an instant. Later, as shown in Eye of the Maelstrom, he explains he would never allow himself to be scanned by any player because capturing Creatures’ Code, besides their bodies and abilities, would also capture their memories and knowledge.

Combat

Being a Strategist, Najarin is rarely seen in battle and when he does he often prefers Air Attacks like Airsault, being most of the times boosted up by a Torwegg.

Najarin is a Muge and as a master of the Mugicians he’s capable of returning an already used and/or discarded Mugic Card back to the game.

The Fluidmorphers’ Foe

warlockss' DeviantArt

During the M’arrillian Invasion, really powerful Creatures entered the fray, some with much stronger innate abilities than most of the Creatures of any Tribe.

Najarin put his knowledge on Mugicians to the test and learned how to counter-attack the Fluidmorphers ability to acquire a Mugicians when successfully hitting a Water Attack for his own benefit. Comes into play the Fluidmorphers’ Foe, an OverWorld Hero capable of receiving Mugicians whenever the opponent receives as well.

As Fluidmorphers’ Foe, Najarin is capable of accumulating enormous amounts of Mugicians when battling against Fluidmorphers and sided to all of this Mugical prowess he’s now able to expend them to increase his squadmates’ Energy as well as completely negate M’arrillians’ Mugics.

High Muge of the Lake
Google Images

After all the marvels I've seen him perform, I still don't know the limits of Najarin's power... or if there are any!

Maxxor

With the Ascension of Kaizeph, the City of the Elements, even though Najarin remains a reliable and loyal advisor, he officially gives up his role as Strategist to devote himself to the life of a Muge. Committed, he’s becomes even wiser and with his improved Mugical knowledge he learns to reduce the cost of Mugics cast. Comes into play the High Muge of the Lake.

Much because of how effective Najarin was in adapting himself to the M’arrillian threat, he’s now capable of mastering the Water Element himself. And as a High Muge he becomes the OverWorld link to Unda, the Servant of Water.

Younger
Google Images

Little did he know then that his name would become synonymous with history itself

During the Secrets of the Lost City, Major Tom defeats CodeMaster Hotekk and together with his friends discovers that the scan of Dranakis Threshold he won as a reward was, in fact, a portal to Ancient Perim.

A while later, after a shocking discover that they ended up in the village of the early days of Kiru City, Tom met Najarin for the first time. Much younger, with a softer expression and normal cheeks, a small dark-brown moustache instead of the long beard and a shoulder-length hair partially hidden by the hood of a much humble clothes without all the jewelleries and heavy make-up.

Even at that time, Najarin was already a wise Muge well versed in the Mugics matter serving as Counsellor of the Tribe Leader Kiru and mentor of his protégé Afjak.

The Animation

In the Animation, for several times Najarin played a critical role in the future of the OverWorld and even all of Perim.

Along with Intress and Tom Majors, he helped Maxxor to recover from the Decomposition Mugic cast by Lord Van Bloot during Fallen Hero arch that was draining all of his Disciplines. He also helped Sobtjek, Zhade, Ubliqun and Ario to fulfil their mission on the Maelstrom Lands during the events of Eye of the Maelstrom which, if failed, could distune their or even all Mugics indefinitely.

During the M’arrillian Invasion, Najarin saved a group of OverWorlder fighters after Tangath Toborn trusted an overconfident — and egocentric — Hune Marquard and his not-so-great knowledge about Mugics in Putting the Muge in Mugic. But his most important intervention was against the infiltration plan to the City of M’arr and kidnapping of Aa’une created by the brainwashed Tartarek.

In Secrets of the Lost City, Najarin had to give up any chance of reuniting with Afjak again by destroying the Dranakis Threshold in order to prevent Lord Van Bloot and his army of rebels from rewriting the whole history of Perim.
